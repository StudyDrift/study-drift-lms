## 1. Problem Statement

Instructors at scale face an impossible grading load: short-answer questions, essays, and file submissions require human evaluation, but the volume makes timely, consistent feedback unachievable. Lextures can already generate rubrics (`assignment_rubric_ai.rs`) but does not use them to score actual submissions. Adding AI-assisted grading with a mandatory instructor approval queue transforms rubric generation from a planning tool into an end-to-end feedback accelerator, while keeping instructors in the loop for every grade that reaches a student.

Cross-reference: §3.4 moderated grading workflow provides the multi-grader reconciliation that instructor approval in this plan integrates with.

## 2. Goals

- Automatically score free-text and file (PDF/DOCX) submissions against the assignment rubric using an LLM.
- Surface AI-suggested scores and feedback in an instructor approval queue.
- Require explicit instructor approval before any AI score reaches the student.
- Feed identified misconceptions from wrong answers to the learner misconception store (§19.4).
- Apply PII redaction to all submission text before sending to the LLM.

## 3. Non-Goals

- Auto-publishing AI grades without instructor approval.
- Grading code-execution submissions (that is §2.4).
- Grading handwritten submissions (that is §19.7).
- Replacing the moderated grading reconciliation workflow of §3.4 — this plan adds an AI grader as one grader in that workflow.

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As an instructor with 80 short-answer submissions**, I want AI to draft a score and feedback for each so that I can review and approve them 3× faster than writing from scratch.
- **As an instructor**, I want to be able to override the AI score and see the AI's reasoning before deciding so that I remain confident in the final grade.
- **As a student**, I want AI-drafted feedback to be substantively helpful, not generic, and I want to know if it was AI-drafted when I receive it.
- **As an org admin**, I want all AI-graded submissions flagged in the gradebook so that auditors can distinguish AI-assisted grades from purely human grades.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The system MUST automatically enqueue AI grading for every new free-text or file submission on assignments that have a rubric and where the instructor has enabled AI-assisted grading.
- **FR-2.** AI grading MUST be performed asynchronously via the background job queue; submission endpoints MUST return immediately without waiting for AI grading.
- **FR-3.** The AI grader MUST produce: a score per rubric criterion, a total score, a feedback text (200–500 words), and a confidence score (0–1).
- **FR-4.** AI-suggested grades MUST be placed in an instructor approval queue; they MUST NOT be visible to students until the instructor approves or overrides.
- **FR-5.** The instructor approval queue MUST allow: approve as-is, edit score/feedback then approve, reject and grade manually.
- **FR-6.** All approved grades (whether AI-origin or not) MUST be flagged in the gradebook with `graded_by_ai: true/false` for audit purposes.
- **FR-7.** All submission content MUST pass through the PII redaction proxy (§19.11) before being sent to the LLM.
- **FR-8.** The AI grader MUST extract misconception signals (incorrect concept claims) and store them per §19.4.
- **FR-9.** The system MUST display a disclosure to students on approved AI-assisted grades: "This feedback was drafted by AI and reviewed by your instructor."
- **FR-10.** The instructor MUST be able to disable AI-assisted grading per-assignment.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — AI grading job MUST complete within 60 s of submission for text submissions ≤ 2 000 words; file submissions ≤ 10 MB MUST complete within 3 min.
- **Security** — AI grading uses the instructor's org model governance settings (§19.10); submission content is org-private; approval queue accessible only to instructors of the course.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — Student submissions are FERPA-protected education records; AI grading constitutes automated decision-making (GDPR Art. 22 — instructor approval gate satisfies the "meaningful human oversight" requirement); AI usage disclosed to students.
- **Accessibility** — Approval queue interface fully keyboard navigable; bulk approve action with ARIA confirmation dialog.
- **Scalability** — Job queue partitioned per course; 200 simultaneous grading jobs per instance supported.
- **Reliability** — If AI grading fails (provider error), submission falls back to manual grading queue with a notification to the instructor.
- **Observability** — `ai_grading_jobs_total{status}`, `ai_grading_latency_ms`, `ai_grading_approval_rate` (what % of AI grades are approved vs. overridden); token usage per job.
- **Maintainability** — AI grading service in `server/src/services/ai_grading.rs`; approval queue route in `server/src/routes/grading.rs`.
- **Internationalization** — AI-generated feedback in instructor's configured language.
- **Backward compatibility** — Manual grading flow unchanged; AI grading opt-in per assignment.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* a student submits a 300-word short answer, *When* AI grading completes, *Then* the instructor sees the submission in the approval queue with score, rubric breakdown, and feedback draft within 60 s.
- **AC-2.** *Given* an AI grade is in the approval queue, *When* the instructor approves without editing, *Then* the student's gradebook shows the score and the grade is flagged `graded_by_ai: true`.
- **AC-3.** *Given* an AI grade is in the approval queue, *When* the instructor reduces the score and clicks approve, *Then* the student receives the instructor-modified score (not the AI original).
- **AC-4.** *Given* a submission containing a student's name and email, *When* the PII proxy processes it, *Then* the prompt sent to the LLM has name and email replaced with tokens.
- **AC-5.** *Given* the AI grading provider returns an error, *When* the job fails, *Then* the submission moves to the manual grading queue and the instructor receives an email notification.
- **AC-6.** *Given* a student views an approved AI-assisted grade, *When* the feedback is displayed, *Then* the disclosure "This feedback was drafted by AI and reviewed by your instructor" is visible.

## 8. Data Model

```sql
-- 0XX_ai_grading.sql
CREATE TYPE ai_grade_status AS ENUM ('pending', 'completed', 'failed', 'approved', 'overridden', 'rejected');

CREATE TABLE ai_grade_suggestions (
  id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  submission_id   UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES assignment_submissions(id),
  status          ai_grade_status NOT NULL DEFAULT 'pending',
  rubric_scores   JSONB,        -- { criterion_id: { score, max_score, rationale } }
  total_score     NUMERIC,
  feedback_text   TEXT,
  confidence      NUMERIC CHECK (confidence BETWEEN 0 AND 1),
  model_id        TEXT,
  token_count     INT,
  misconceptions  JSONB,        -- [{ concept, description, severity }]
  reviewed_by     UUID REFERENCES users(id),
  reviewed_at     TIMESTAMPTZ,
  final_score     NUMERIC,      -- instructor-set value (may differ from total_score)
  graded_by_ai    BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT true,
  created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);
CREATE INDEX ai_grade_suggestions_submission ON ai_grade_suggestions(submission_id);
CREATE INDEX ai_grade_suggestions_status ON ai_grade_suggestions(status);
```

## 11. AI / ML Considerations

- **Model**: Claude-3.5-Sonnet via OpenRouter; long-context model for file submissions.
- **Prompt**: System prompt includes full rubric, assignment description, sample responses at each grade band; stored in DB per §19.10.
- **File submissions**: PDF/DOCX extracted to text server-side before LLM call (pdfium / docx-rs).
- **Eval metric**: Instructor override rate (target <25%); inter-rater reliability between AI and instructor scores (Cohen's κ ≥ 0.6); ARES eval framework for RAG-grounded grading.
- **Fallback**: Provider failure → manual queue; low confidence (<0.6) → flagged for mandatory review.
- **Model card**: `docs/ai/grading-model-card.md`.
- **Cost**: ~3 000–6 000 tokens per submission; §19.14 budget controls.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Instructors rubber-stamp AI grades without review | H | H | Approval queue UX designed for active review; low-confidence alerts |
| LLM produces biased grading (race/gender correlations) | M | H | Bias audit in eval harness (§19.13); PII redaction reduces bias signals |
| GDPR Art. 22 — automated decision-making challenge | M | M | Instructor approval gate = meaningful human oversight; documented in policy |
| File extraction fails for complex PDFs | M | M | Fallback to manual queue; log extraction failures |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- Feature flag: `ai_grading_enabled` (OpenFeature, default `false`).
- Phase 1: Opt-in for HE instructors only (fewer COPPA constraints); eval harness baseline.
- Phase 2: K-12 pilot with parental consent notices updated for AI grading.
- Phase 3: GA after override-rate and bias-audit thresholds met.

## 16. Test Plan

- **Unit** — Rubric score parsing; confidence thresholds; misconception extraction; PII proxy call.
- **Integration** — Submit → job queued → AI grades → approval queue visible → approve → gradebook updated.
- **End-to-end** — Playwright: instructor opens queue → reviews AI suggestion → overrides score → approves → student sees grade.
- **Security** — Student cannot access approval queue; cross-course submission access blocked.
- **AI eval** — 100 golden submissions with instructor-assigned scores; κ ≥ 0.6 pass criterion; bias audit across demographic proxies.
- **Performance** — 200 concurrent grading jobs; p95 ≤ 60 s for text submissions.
