## 1. Problem Statement

`clients/web/src/pages/Privacy.tsx` and `clients/web/src/pages/Terms.tsx` exist as React pages, but their content is unverified for legal accuracy and completeness. The MISSING_FEATURES audit lists this as THIN (works but incomplete). An LMS handling student PII, AI-generated content, and institutional data cannot pass procurement review with skeleton or boilerplate legal pages. FERPA, GDPR, COPPA, CCPA, and AI disclosure requirements each mandate specific disclosures. This plan defines what content must be in the documents and the process to verify and publish them.

## 2. Goals

- Ensure Privacy Policy covers all legally required disclosures: FERPA (US), GDPR (EU), COPPA (under-13), CCPA/CPRA (California), AI data use (§10.17), sub-processor list (cross-ref §20.2).
- Ensure Terms of Service cover: acceptable use, content ownership, DMCA, age requirements, AI-generated content ownership, arbitration/dispute resolution.
- Pages must be versioned (effective date visible) and archived.
- Link to Trust Center (§20.2) from Privacy Policy.
- Make both pages machine-readable (JSON-LD structured data for SEO).

## 3. Non-Goals

- Data Subject Access Request (DSAR) workflow — that is §10.3.
- Consent banner/cookie management — related but separate.
- DPA template for institutional customers — covered by §10.5.
- Building a compliance management system.

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As a K-12 district IT admin reviewing a vendor**, I want to read the Privacy Policy and confirm it explicitly addresses FERPA and COPPA so that I can recommend Lextures to our legal team.
- **As a student in the EU**, I want to read the Privacy Policy and find my GDPR rights (access, erasure, portability) clearly described so that I know how to exercise them.
- **As a parent of an under-13 student**, I want to understand how my child's data (including audio submissions) is handled before granting consent.
- **As an instructor**, I want to understand who owns the AI-generated content I create using Lextures tools.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The Privacy Policy page MUST explicitly address: FERPA compliance (for students and parents), COPPA (for under-13 students), GDPR rights (EU users: access, erasure, portability, objection, restriction), CCPA/CPRA "do not sell/share" (California users), AI data use (models used, data sent to LLMs, opt-out mechanism), sub-processor list or link to it (§20.2), data retention periods, security measures summary.
- **FR-2.** The Terms of Service page MUST explicitly address: acceptable use, minimum age requirements (13 for students, with parental consent for under-13 subject to COPPA), content ownership (user-generated vs. AI-generated), DMCA policy, dispute resolution and arbitration clause, governing law and jurisdiction, AI feature terms (accuracy disclaimer, no professional advice).
- **FR-3.** Both pages MUST display: effective date, version number, and a "History of changes" link.
- **FR-4.** Both pages MUST be accessible at `/privacy` and `/terms` without requiring login.
- **FR-5.** The Privacy Policy MUST include a contact address for privacy inquiries: `privacy@lextures.com` (or equivalent).
- **FR-6.** Both pages MUST comply with WCAG 2.1 AA (readable font size, proper heading structure, keyboard-navigable table of contents).
- **FR-7.** The Privacy Policy MUST list all AI model providers (OpenRouter, Anthropic, OpenAI, etc.) as sub-processors and explain what data is sent to them.
- **FR-8.** The system MUST display a "Privacy Policy has been updated" banner to returning users when the effective date changes, with a "I acknowledge" action.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — Pages MUST be server-side rendered or statically generated (not behind auth) so they are crawlable and load fast (< 1 s on 3G).
- **Security** — Pages are read-only; no forms except email contact link. No tracking pixels on Privacy page (GDPR irony). Cookie banner on Terms/Privacy must itself be consent-legal.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — The Privacy Policy is the primary compliance document; must be reviewed by qualified legal counsel before publication; effective date must change on any material modification.
- **Accessibility** — WCAG 2.1 AA; reading level SHOULD be ≤ grade 10 for COPPA-relevant sections; headings create navigable structure.
- **Scalability** — Static content, trivially scalable.
- **Reliability** — Pages must be reachable even during platform maintenance (serve from CDN).
- **Observability** — Track page views for "Privacy Policy read" as a compliance signal.
- **Maintainability** — Content managed as Markdown/MDX in `clients/web/src/content/legal/`; rendered by `Privacy.tsx` and `Terms.tsx`.
- **Internationalization** — English-language documents are legally binding; consider providing non-binding translations for major markets (EU, LATAM) as supplementary links.
- **Backward compatibility** — Existing `/privacy` and `/terms` URLs preserved.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* a district IT admin opens the Privacy Policy, *When* they search for "FERPA", *Then* they find at least one section explicitly describing FERPA rights and how Lextures complies.
- **AC-2.** *Given* a user in Germany opens the Privacy Policy, *When* they look for their rights, *Then* they find a clearly labeled "Your Rights Under GDPR" section covering access, erasure, portability, and objection.
- **AC-3.** *Given* the Privacy Policy lists AI sub-processors, *When* a user reads it, *Then* they find the names of LLM providers (Anthropic, OpenAI) and a description of what data is shared with them.
- **AC-4.** *Given* an axe-core accessibility scan runs on the Privacy Policy page, *When* the scan completes, *Then* zero critical or serious WCAG 2.1 AA violations are found.
- **AC-5.** *Given* the Privacy Policy effective date is updated, *When* a returning authenticated user logs in, *Then* they see a banner acknowledging the update, and their dismissal is recorded.
- **AC-6.** *Given* a legal counsel review identifies a required change, *When* the MDX content is updated and deployed, *Then* the effective date field on the page updates and a `privacy_policy_update_acknowledged` event is required from existing users.

## 8. Data Model

```sql
-- 0XX_legal_acknowledgements.sql
CREATE TABLE user_legal_acknowledgements (
  id             UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  user_id        UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id),
  document       TEXT NOT NULL,    -- 'privacy_policy' | 'terms_of_service'
  version        TEXT NOT NULL,    -- e.g. '2026-04-17'
  acknowledged_at TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);
CREATE UNIQUE INDEX ula_user_doc_version ON user_legal_acknowledgements(user_id, document, version);
```

## 9. API Surface

```
POST /api/legal/acknowledge  { document: 'privacy_policy', version: '2026-04-17' } -> 204
GET  /api/legal/pending      -> { documents: [{ document, version, effective_date }] }
```

Both legal pages are statically served — no API for reading content.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Legal requirements change (new state laws) | H | M | Quarterly legal review cadence; version-controlled MDX |
| AI disclosure requirements tighten (EU AI Act) | M | M | Monitor EU AI Act compliance dates; dedicated AI disclosure section easy to update |
| Privacy Policy not reviewed by qualified counsel | M | H | Gate publication on signed-off legal review; track in project board |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- Phase 1: Legal counsel review and redline of current content.
- Phase 2: MDX rewrite incorporating redlines; peer review by product.
- Phase 3: Deploy and update effective date; trigger acknowledgement banner.
- Phase 4: Quarterly review cadence established as recurring process.

## 16. Test Plan

- **Accessibility** — axe-core on both pages; screen-reader check of table of contents navigation.
- **Legal review** — Checklist covering FERPA, GDPR, COPPA, CCPA, AI disclosure, sub-processor list.
- **Manual** — Verify all anchor links work; verify print layout; verify page loads without login.
- **Performance** — Lighthouse score ≥ 90 on both pages.

## 17. Documentation & Training
