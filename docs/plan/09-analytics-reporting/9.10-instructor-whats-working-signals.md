## 1. Problem Statement

Instructors receive grades and engagement data but have no surface that synthesizes these into actionable "what is working well in this course" vs. "what needs attention" signals. Section-level comparisons help instructors understand if their teaching approach is differentially effective compared to other sections or cohorts. Instructors teaching multiple sections of the same course cannot easily see which section is underperforming and why, nor which content elements (assignments, videos, readings) consistently correlate with good outcomes. This feature provides that synthesis layer.

## 2. Goals

- Surface a "Health Dashboard" to instructors showing the course elements that are most correlated with high student outcomes (positive signals) and those correlated with low outcomes or disengagement (negative signals).
- Enable cross-section comparison for instructors who teach multiple sections of the same course.
- Highlight specific content items (videos, readings, assignments) with high completion and strong performance as "working well."
- Recommend specific remediation actions for underperforming items.

## 3. Non-Goals

- Instructor performance evaluation or HR reporting (this is for self-improvement only; no admin visibility into individual instructor signals).
- Prescriptive pedagogical recommendations (signals only; no mandate).
- Cross-institution benchmarking (deferred to a network-effects feature).

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As an instructor teaching 3 sections**, I want to see which section is performing below the others so that I can investigate whether a scheduling or delivery difference explains the gap.
- **As an instructor reviewing a course redesign**, I want to see which new assignments correlate with the highest quiz scores so that I can keep them next semester.
- **As an instructor**, I want a "needs attention" flag on the reading that 80 % of students skip so that I know to revisit its relevance.
- **As a department chair**, I want instructors to have their own improvement insights so that teaching quality conversations are data-informed.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The system MUST compute a "Working Well" list (top 3 course items by: high completion rate × high average score on aligned quiz questions) and a "Needs Attention" list (bottom 3 items by low completion rate or low score correlation).
- **FR-2.** The system MUST provide a cross-section comparison widget for instructors who are the primary instructor of multiple active sections of the same course: shows avg grade, avg completion, and at-risk count per section in a side-by-side table.
- **FR-3.** The system MUST compute a "Difficulty vs. Engagement" scatter plot per course item (x = item difficulty derived from quiz scores, y = time-on-task engagement); items in the low-engagement / high-difficulty quadrant are flagged "Needs redesign."
- **FR-4.** "What's Working" insights MUST refresh weekly (nightly aggregation from grades, engagement, and item analysis data).
- **FR-5.** Instructors MUST be able to dismiss a signal and provide a reason (e.g., "Known — this assignment is intentionally hard") so that dismissed signals do not re-appear.
- **FR-6.** The system SHOULD provide one AI-generated narrative sentence per signal (e.g., "Students who complete Module 3 reading score 15 % higher on Quiz 4") using correlation data as context.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — Insights page loads < 800 ms (pre-computed weekly). Cross-section comparison < 500 ms.
- **Security** — Instructor sees only their own course signals; admin can see all. Signals not visible to students. Cross-section comparison shows only sections where the viewer is the instructor.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — FERPA: signals derived from aggregated student data; individual students not identified in the signal text. GDPR: signals deleted when course data is erased.
- **Accessibility** — Scatter plot has text table fallback. All signal cards keyboard-navigable. WCAG 2.1 AA.
- **Scalability** — Weekly batch computation; signals stored as JSON in `analytics.instructor_insights`.
- **Reliability** — If computation fails, serve previous week's signals with "as of {date}" label.
- **Observability** — Metrics: `instructor_insights_computed_total`, `signal_dismissed_total`. Log course_id, signal_type, trigger_reason.
- **Maintainability** — Insights engine in `server/src/services/instructor_insights.rs` (new); route in `server/src/routes/reports.rs`.
- **Internationalization** — AI narrative sentences generated in the instructor's interface language. Signal labels externalised.
- **Backward compatibility** — Additive feature; no changes to existing reports.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* a course has 5 assignments with grade and engagement data *When* the weekly insights job runs *Then* the "Working Well" list contains the top 3 items by completion × score composite and the "Needs Attention" list the bottom 3.
- **AC-2.** *Given* an instructor teaches Sections A and B of CS 101 *When* they open the cross-section comparison *Then* a table shows Section A (avg 82 %, 3 at-risk) vs Section B (avg 74 %, 8 at-risk) side by side.
- **AC-3.** *Given* a reading has 85 % skip rate and a low quiz score correlation *When* it appears in the scatter plot *Then* it is in the "Low engagement / Low outcome" quadrant with a "Needs redesign" flag.
- **AC-4.** *Given* an instructor dismisses a "Needs Attention" signal and provides reason "Known issue — remediated next semester" *When* the weekly job next runs *Then* the signal does not reappear and the dismissal reason is logged.
- **AC-5.** *Given* an AI narrative sentence is requested for a signal *When* the sentence is generated *Then* it references only aggregate statistics (e.g., "X % of students…") and no individual student names.
- **AC-6.** *Given* a screen-reader user navigates the scatter plot *When* they reach it *Then* a table is available listing each item with its difficulty and engagement values.

## 8. Data Model

```sql
-- server/migrations/093_instructor_insights.sql
CREATE TABLE analytics.instructor_insights (
  id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  course_id       UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES course.courses(id),
  week_of         DATE NOT NULL,
  working_well    JSONB NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  needs_attention JSONB NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  scatter_data    JSONB NOT NULL DEFAULT '[]',
  generated_at    TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  UNIQUE (course_id, week_of)
);

CREATE TABLE analytics.dismissed_signals (
  id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  course_id       UUID NOT NULL,
  signal_key      TEXT NOT NULL,
  dismissed_by    UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES user_account(id),
  reason          TEXT,
  dismissed_at    TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now(),
  UNIQUE (course_id, signal_key)
);
```

## 9. API Surface

| Method | Path | Auth | Description |

## 10. UI / UX

- New "What's Working" tab in the course analytics page (`Reports.tsx`).
- Two column layout: "Working Well" (green cards with checkmark icons) and "Needs Attention" (amber cards with warning icons).
- Each signal card: item name, metric summary (e.g., "92 % completion, avg quiz +12 pp"), AI narrative sentence, "Dismiss" button.
- Cross-section comparison panel: compact table if instructor has > 1 section; hidden if single section.
- Scatter plot: x-axis "Difficulty (quiz avg)" 0–100, y-axis "Engagement (avg time on task)" 0–max. Quadrant labels. Hover tooltip with item name and exact values.
- Accessibility: signal cards have `role="article"` with `aria-label`. Scatter plot has `<details><summary>Show data table</summary>` fallback. Dismiss button labeled.
- Mobile: cards stack vertically; scatter plot scrollable horizontally.
- i18n keys: `insights.workingWell`, `insights.needsAttention`, `insights.dismiss`, `insights.crossSection`, `insights.refreshed`.

## 11. AI / ML Considerations

- **Model:** small/fast LLM call (Haiku-class) for one-sentence narrative per signal using only aggregate stats as context.
- **Prompt:** "Given that {pct}% of students completed [item] and scored {avg} on the related quiz, write one encouraging/actionable sentence for an instructor."
- **Fallback:** if LLM fails, show computed statistics without narrative.
- **Cost budget:** 7 signals per course per week × ~50 tokens each = negligible; batch for all courses Sunday night.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Signals are trivially obvious (e.g., "students who skip everything do worse") | M | M | Minimum correlation threshold (r > 0.3) before signaling; calibrate with pilot instructors |
| Instructor uses signals to evaluate peer instructors | L | H | Signals visible only to the owning instructor, not department chairs or admin |
| AI narrative is inaccurate or misleading | M | M | Narrative uses only computed aggregate numbers; human review of prompt in red-team |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- **Flag:** `FEATURE_INSTRUCTOR_INSIGHTS=true` (default false).
- **Sequencing:** insights computation engine → schema → API → UI tab → AI narrative (optional, last) → enable flag.
- **GA criteria:** working-well and needs-attention lists compute correctly on pilot courses; cross-section comparison functional; scatter plot readable.
- **Rollback:** disable flag; Reports.tsx reverts to previous tabs.

## 16. Test Plan

- **Unit** — Composite score computation; quadrant classification; cross-section comparison aggregation.
- **Integration** — Seeded course with 5 items varying in completion/score: verify top-3 and bottom-3 correctly classified.
- **End-to-end** — Playwright: seed grade and engagement data, trigger insights refresh, open What's Working tab, verify signal cards.
- **Security** — Instructor A cannot view Instructor B's course insights. Student cannot access insights.
- **Accessibility** — axe scan of insights tab; scatter data table available.
- **Manual exploratory** — Cross-section comparison with 3 sections; AI narrative quality review.

## 17. Documentation & Training

- Instructor docs: "Understanding your course health signals — how working-well and needs-attention signals are calculated."
- Internal: signal computation algorithm documentation; correlation threshold rationale.
- Help center: "What does 'Needs redesign' mean for a course item?"

## 18. Open Questions

1. Should cross-section comparison be opt-in (some instructors may not want their section compared to peers)?
2. Should we eventually aggregate working-well signals across the platform to create a "what works in this subject area" benchmark?
3. Should the AI narrative be a one-sentence insight or a bulleted list of 2–3 recommendations?
