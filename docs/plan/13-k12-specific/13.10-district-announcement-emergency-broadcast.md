## 1. Problem Statement

K-12 districts require a multi-tier broadcast communication system: (a) routine district-wide and school-wide announcements (policy updates, event reminders, schedule changes), and (b) emergency broadcasts (school lockdowns, weather closures, safety alerts) that must reach all users immediately with read receipts. Lextures currently has a course-level feed and an inbox, but no cross-course, school-wide, or district-wide broadcast capability. Without this, districts must maintain a separate mass-notification tool (SchoolMessenger, ParentSquare, Remind) alongside Lextures, fragmenting communication and reducing LMS adoption. An emergency-broadcast capability with guaranteed delivery confirmation is increasingly expected after school-safety mandates in many states.

## 2. Goals

- Provide district admins and school principals with a broadcast message tool that reaches all users in a school or district simultaneously.
- Support multiple channels: in-app banner, email (§6.2), push notification (§6.3).
- Implement an emergency-broadcast mode with higher visibility, read receipts, and delivery confirmation.
- Allow broadcast messages to be targeted by audience: district-wide, school-wide, grade level, role (staff only, parents only, all).
- Maintain a broadcast archive accessible to admins.

## 3. Non-Goals

- Two-way emergency communication (e.g., student SOS button) — safety-hardware integration scope.
- SMS/phone-call mass notification (third-party telephony like SchoolMessenger; not v1).
- Integration with E911 or IPAWS (federal emergency alert system) — requires FCC licensing.
- Course-level announcement thread (existing course feed handles that).

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As a district superintendent**, I want to send a "School closed tomorrow due to snow" message to all parents, students, and staff across all 12 schools at once so that everyone gets the information through the platform they use for coursework.
- **As a school principal**, I want to send an emergency lockdown notification to all staff and students in my building immediately, with a confirmation list showing who has seen it, so that I can account for acknowledgment.
- **As a parent**, I want to receive urgent school notifications as both an in-app banner and an email so that I don't miss critical communications even if I'm not actively using the app.
- **As a district admin**, I want to view a delivery report showing what percentage of recipients opened each broadcast so that I can evaluate communication effectiveness.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The system MUST provide a broadcast composer restricted to `district_admin`, `principal`, and `school_admin` roles, with audience selection: district-wide, school-wide, grade-level band, role filter (all / staff / parents / students).
- **FR-2.** Broadcast messages MUST be delivered via: (a) in-app persistent banner (dismissible for normal; non-dismissible for emergency), (b) email (§6.2), (c) push notification (§6.3).
- **FR-3.** Emergency broadcasts MUST be flagged with `type = 'emergency'`, which triggers: non-dismissible banner, highest-priority notification, and an explicit "I acknowledge" button that records a read receipt.
- **FR-4.** Delivery reports MUST show per-message: total recipients, delivery rate by channel (email, push, in-app), read rate (in-app banner acknowledged), and a list of users who have NOT yet acknowledged an emergency message.
- **FR-5.** Broadcast messages MUST be stored in a broadcast archive accessible to district admins with full message history, delivery stats, and sender identity.
- **FR-6.** The broadcast composer MUST support rich text (via TipTap), file attachments (links only, no binary attachments in v1), and a scheduled send time (up to 7 days in advance).
- **FR-7.** Emergency broadcast delivery MUST use a high-priority background job path (§17.3) separate from regular notification queues to ensure < 60 s delivery to all in-app sessions.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — Normal broadcast to 5,000 users: email/push queued within 30 s; in-app banner displayed within 5 s for active sessions. Emergency broadcast in-app: < 60 s for 10,000 active sessions.
- **Security** — Only authorized roles can send broadcasts; authz enforced in `server/src/authz.rs`. Broadcast content is logged in `user_audit` for FERPA compliance. Emergency acknowledgment data is FERPA-protected.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — FERPA: broadcast delivery reports contain user identity (who acknowledged); access restricted to school officials. COPPA: broadcasts to under-13 students require appropriate content. CAN-SPAM / CASL: email broadcasts must include unsubscribe mechanism (except emergency). California AB 2943 / state school-communication laws may apply.
- **Accessibility** — Emergency banner must meet WCAG 2.1 AA SC 1.4.3 contrast; the "I acknowledge" button must be keyboard-accessible and screen-reader announced as `role="alertdialog"` (WCAG SC 4.1.3). In-app banner must not obscure main content (WCAG SC 1.4.4 Reflow).
- **Scalability** — Broadcasts to 10,000 users; background job fan-out with batched email sending (max 500/batch via email provider).
- **Reliability** — Broadcast job is idempotent; re-running does not send duplicate messages. Failed individual deliveries logged without blocking other recipients.
- **Observability** — Metrics: `broadcast_messages_sent_per_day`, `emergency_broadcast_ack_rate`, `email_delivery_failure_rate`. Alert: emergency broadcast ACK rate < 80% after 10 minutes.
- **Maintainability** — Broadcast module in `server/src/routes/broadcasts.rs`; notification fan-out in `server/src/services/notification_fanout.rs`.
- **Internationalization** — Broadcast content authored in any language; translation via §6.10 (future); i18n keys for UI chrome.
- **Backward compatibility** — New feature; no existing tables affected.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* a district admin sends a broadcast to "All staff" at District X, *When* the job completes, *Then* all users with `role = 'instructor'` or `'admin'` in any school in District X have received an in-app banner notification within 5 seconds.
- **AC-2.** *Given* a principal sends an emergency broadcast, *When* a student in that school opens the app, *Then* a non-dismissible red banner with "EMERGENCY" label appears with an "I acknowledge" button; dismissing without acknowledging is not possible.
- **AC-3.** *Given* 100 students received an emergency broadcast, *When* the principal views the delivery report, *Then* the report shows total recipients, how many acknowledged, and lists the names of the 15 who have not yet acknowledged.
- **AC-4.** *Given* a NVDA user receives the emergency banner, *When* the banner appears, *Then* NVDA announces "Emergency: [message title] — press Enter to acknowledge" and focus moves to the "I acknowledge" button.
- **AC-5.** *Given* a broadcast is scheduled for tomorrow at 8 AM, *When* the scheduled time arrives, *Then* the message is sent automatically without manual intervention.
- **AC-6.** *Given* an admin user views the broadcast archive, *When* there are 5 past broadcasts, *Then* all 5 are listed with sender, timestamp, audience, and delivery stats.

## 8. Data Model

```sql
-- server/migrations/089_broadcasts.sql
CREATE TABLE broadcasts (
    id            UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    org_id        UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES organizations(id),
    school_id     UUID,                     -- NULL = district-wide
    sender_id     UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id),
    type          TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'announcement',  -- announcement | emergency
    audience      JSONB NOT NULL,           -- {scope: district|school|grade, roles: [...]}
    subject       TEXT NOT NULL,
    body          TEXT NOT NULL,
    scheduled_at  TIMESTAMPTZ,
    sent_at       TIMESTAMPTZ,
    status        TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'draft',  -- draft | queued | sent
    created_at    TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT now()
);
CREATE TABLE broadcast_receipts (
    id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
    broadcast_id    UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES broadcasts(id),
    user_id         UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES users(id),
    channel         TEXT NOT NULL,  -- in_app | email | push
    delivered_at    TIMESTAMPTZ,
    acknowledged_at TIMESTAMPTZ,    -- for emergency type
    UNIQUE(broadcast_id, user_id, channel)
);
```

## 9. API Surface

- `POST /api/broadcasts` — create/send broadcast; district_admin/principal.
- `GET /api/broadcasts` — list broadcasts; admin.
- `GET /api/broadcasts/:id/delivery-report` — delivery stats; admin.
- `POST /api/broadcasts/:id/acknowledge` — user acknowledges emergency; authenticated user.
- `GET /api/me/broadcasts` — in-app banner queue for current user.

## 10. UI / UX

- **New pages:** `clients/web/src/pages/admin/BroadcastComposer.tsx`, `BroadcastArchive.tsx`, `BroadcastDeliveryReport.tsx`.
- **New component:** `clients/web/src/components/layout/BroadcastBanner.tsx` — persistent banner mounted in app layout.
- **Emergency styling:** Red background, bold "EMERGENCY" label, `role="alertdialog"`, non-dismissible until acknowledged.
- **User flows:** 1. Admin composes broadcast → selects audience → sends or schedules. 2. User sees banner → acknowledges. 3. Admin reviews delivery report.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Emergency broadcast not delivered within 60 s due to email queue backlog | M | H | Separate high-priority job queue for emergency type; in-app delivery bypasses email queue |
| Non-emergency broadcasts perceived as spam | M | M | Configurable notification preferences; unsubscribe from announcements (not emergency) |
| Broadcast to wrong audience (e.g., district-wide when only school intended) | M | H | Confirmation dialog with recipient count preview before send |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- Feature flag: `ff_broadcasts` (default `false`).
- Sequencing: Data model → fan-out job → in-app banner → email → push → composer UI → flip flag.
- Pilot: One district admin sends 3 test broadcasts to internal team.
- GA: All 6 ACs pass; emergency delivery time verified < 60 s.

## 16. Test Plan

- **Unit:** Audience resolver; fan-out job batching; receipt upsert.

- **Security:** Non-admin role cannot create broadcasts.
- **Accessibility:** Emergency banner `role="alertdialog"`; NVDA announcement.
- **Performance:** Fan-out to 10,000 users < 60 s in-app.
