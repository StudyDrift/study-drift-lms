## 1. Problem Statement

ISO/IEC 27001:2022 is the international standard for Information Security Management Systems (ISMS) and is the primary security certification required by non-US higher-education institutions, government-affiliated universities, and many European K-12 consortia. ISO/IEC 27701:2019 is the Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) extension that maps to GDPR Article 25 ("data protection by design") and is increasingly required in EU procurement. Neither certification exists for Lextures. Without ISO 27001, the international HE market — which represents the majority of global higher-education enrollment — is effectively closed.

## 2. Goals

- Implement and document an ISMS (Information Security Management System) conforming to ISO/IEC 27001:2022.
- Extend the ISMS with a Privacy Information Management System (PIMS) conforming to ISO/IEC 27701:2019.
- Achieve Stage 1 (documentation review) and Stage 2 (implementation audit) certification from an accredited body (UKAS/DAkkS accredited).
- Establish continuous improvement processes, annual management reviews, and internal audit cadence.
- Leverage evidence overlap with SOC 2 Type II (10.9) to minimize duplicate effort.

## 3. Non-Goals

- ISO 27017 (cloud-specific controls) and ISO 27018 (PII in public cloud) — future v2 scope.
- ISO 9001 (quality management) — separate program.
- Full operational ISMS for Lextures' entire corporate entity if it is an early-stage startup; a scoped ISMS covering the Lextures LMS SaaS service is sufficient for v1 certification.

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As an EU university procurement officer**, I want to see Lextures' ISO 27001 certificate so that I can approve the vendor under our IT security policy.
- **As an international HE CIO**, I want to review the Statement of Applicability (SoA) to verify which ISO 27001 Annex A controls are implemented and which are excluded with justification.
- **As a Lextures ISMS owner**, I want documented information security policies and procedures so that I can manage risk systematically and respond to certification audits.
- **As a GDPR Data Protection Officer**, I want Lextures' ISO 27701 certification to demonstrate that GDPR Article 25 (data protection by design and by default) is operationalized.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The organization MUST define an ISMS scope statement covering the Lextures LMS SaaS service, including all system components referenced in `iac/demo/` and `iac/production/`.
- **FR-2.** The system MUST implement all applicable ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A controls (93 controls across 4 themes: Organizational, People, Physical, Technological); excluded controls MUST be justified in the Statement of Applicability (SoA).
- **FR-3.** The organization MUST conduct an annual ISO 27001 internal audit and management review; findings MUST be tracked in `compliance.iso_audit_findings`.
- **FR-4.** The system MUST implement the ISO/IEC 27701:2019 additional controls for PII processing (Annex B — PII Controller, Annex C — PII Processor) mapped to GDPR obligations.
- **FR-5.** The organization MUST maintain a risk register with identified information security risks, treatment decisions, and residual risk acceptance records.
- **FR-6.** All staff with access to production systems MUST complete annual ISO 27001-aligned security awareness training; completion MUST be recorded.
- **FR-7.** The system MUST support a documented supplier/sub-processor security review process consistent with ISO 27001 A.5.22 (Information security in supplier relationships).

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — No performance requirements for documentation artifacts.
- **Security** — ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Annex A Technological controls include: A.8.2 (Privileged access rights), A.8.5 (Secure authentication), A.8.7 (Protection against malware), A.8.8 (Management of technical vulnerabilities), A.8.9 (Configuration management), A.8.15 (Logging), A.8.24 (Use of cryptography). These map directly to engineering controls in `server/src/authz.rs`, `server/src/http_auth.rs`, `iac/production/`.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — ISO/IEC 27001:2022 (replaces 2013 version); ISO/IEC 27701:2019 (PIMS extension); GDPR Article 25 (data protection by design). The 27701 PII controller controls map to GDPR Articles 13–22; processor controls map to Article 28. NIST 800-53 controls overlap with 27001 Annex A; a mapping table MUST be maintained. OWASP ASVS 4.0 serves as a technical-implementation reference for Technological controls.
- **Availability** — ISMS requires a Business Continuity Management policy (ISO 27001 A.5.29, A.5.30); BCM plans MUST be tested annually. Cross-link: plan 10.15 (Backup/restore).
- **Reliability** — Internal audit and management review cycle MUST be on a defined annual schedule.
- **Observability** — All audit findings and non-conformities MUST be tracked with corrective action due dates in `compliance.iso_audit_findings`. Cross-link: plan 10.11 (Admin audit log) and plan 17.7 (Observability).
- **Maintainability** — ISMS documentation in `docs/isms/`; version-controlled; reviewed annually.
- **Internationalization** — ISMS documentation in English; certification bodies in non-English markets accept English documentation.
- **Backward compatibility** — ISMS implementation builds on existing security controls documented in `docs/SECURITY.md`.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* the Stage 1 audit is conducted, *when* the certification body reviews the ISMS documentation, *then* the Statement of Applicability covers all 93 Annex A controls with justified exclusions and is accepted without major non-conformities.
- **AC-2.** *Given* the Stage 2 audit is conducted, *when* the auditor samples production systems, *then* privileged access is limited to named individuals, MFA is enforced on all production access, and access reviews are evidenced for the prior 12 months.
- **AC-3.** *Given* an information security incident occurs, *when* the incident response procedure is followed, *then* the incident is recorded in `compliance.incidents` (10.9), a root-cause analysis is completed, and corrective actions are tracked in the ISMS.
- **AC-4.** *Given* the ISO 27701 audit verifies PII controller controls, *when* the auditor reviews the DSAR and erasure workflows (10.3), *then* the controls satisfy ISO 27701 Annex B.2.5 (access to PII) and B.2.6 (correction/erasure).
- **AC-5.** *Given* a new sub-processor is added, *when* the supplier security review process runs, *then* a completed security questionnaire or the sub-processor's own ISO 27001/SOC 2 certificate is on file before the sub-processor handles production data.

## 8. Data Model

```sql
-- Migration: 080_iso_isms.sql

CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS compliance.iso_audit_findings (
  id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  audit_cycle     TEXT NOT NULL,  -- e.g. '2026-internal', '2027-stage2'
  finding_type    TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (finding_type IN ('nonconformity','observation','opportunity')),
  iso_clause      TEXT NOT NULL,  -- e.g. '8.2', 'A.8.15'
  description     TEXT NOT NULL,
  status          TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'open' CHECK (status IN ('open','in_progress','closed')),
  corrective_action TEXT,
  due_date        DATE,
  closed_at       TIMESTAMPTZ,
  created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW()
);

CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS compliance.risk_register (
  id              UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  risk_title      TEXT NOT NULL,
  likelihood      INTEGER NOT NULL CHECK (likelihood BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  impact          INTEGER NOT NULL CHECK (impact BETWEEN 1 AND 5),
  treatment       TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (treatment IN ('mitigate','accept','transfer','avoid')),
  residual_score  INTEGER GENERATED ALWAYS AS (likelihood * impact) STORED,
  owner_id        UUID REFERENCES user.users(id),
  review_date     DATE,
  created_at      TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW()
);
```

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Certification body identifies major non-conformities | M | H | Pre-Stage 1 gap assessment with consultant; remediate before formal audit |
| ISO 27001:2022 transition from 2013 version affects existing controls | L | M | Build directly to 2022; no legacy controls to update |
| ISMS documentation becomes stale between audits | M | M | Annual review in calendar; version-controlled in git |
| Staff turnover disrupts ISMS ownership | M | M | Document roles; bus-factor > 1 for all ISMS processes |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- Months 1–3: Gap assessment; draft ISMS policies; risk register populated.
- Months 4–6: Control implementation; internal audit; corrective actions.
- Months 7–9: Stage 1 audit (documentation review).
- Months 10–18: Stage 2 audit (implementation); certification issued.
- Ongoing: annual surveillance audits; 3-year recertification cycle.
