## 1. Problem Statement

The Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), 15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506, and the FTC Rule at 16 CFR Part 312, prohibit operators of websites and online services from collecting personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental consent (VPC). Lextures has no age gate, no minor account flag, no data-minimization mode for under-13 users, and no VPC workflow. A single K-12 district deployment without this feature exposes the operator to FTC civil penalties up to $51,744 per violation per day. The feature is a hard prerequisite for any US K-12 sales motion.

## 2. Goals

- Implement a COPPA-compliant under-13 detection and flagging system.
- Build a verifiable parental consent (VPC) workflow with at least two FTC-approved consent methods.
- Enforce a data-minimization mode that restricts data collection, AI processing, and third-party sharing for minor accounts.
- Provide a parent consent management dashboard to review, modify, or revoke consent.
- Log all consent events for FTC audit purposes.

## 3. Non-Goals

- FERPA parent-access workflow (see 10.1) — COPPA consent and FERPA access are separate legal instruments.
- GDPR child-consent rules for EU users (see 10.3) — COPPA covers US jurisdiction only.
- Age verification beyond self-declared DOB + parental confirmation (biometric verification is out of scope for v1).

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As a parent of a child under 13**, I want to receive a consent notice, review what data will be collected, and give verifiable consent before my child's account is activated.
- **As a district IT admin**, I want to provide school-authorization-based consent in bulk for students whose parents have signed a district-level consent form (16 CFR § 312.5(b)(1) school authorization).
- **As a student under 13**, I want to use the platform only after my parent or district has confirmed consent, and I want the UI to hide features that require unnecessary data collection.
- **As a compliance officer**, I want to audit all consent records and export them on request.
- **As an instructor**, I want to know which of my students are minor accounts so that I apply appropriate data-handling practices.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The system MUST flag any user account where the user's date of birth indicates age < 13 as a `coppa_minor` account (16 CFR § 312.2 definition of "child").
- **FR-2.** The system MUST gate account activation for `coppa_minor` accounts behind verifiable parental consent; the account MUST NOT collect personal information beyond the child's first name and school/org ID until consent is received (16 CFR § 312.5 consent requirements).
- **FR-3.** The system MUST support at least two FTC-recognized VPC methods: (a) a signed consent form submitted by email or upload, and (b) district/school authorization (16 CFR § 312.5(b)(1)) where the institution acts as parent's agent.
- **FR-4.** The system MUST send a consent notice email to the parent email address collected at account creation, describing: operator identity, categories of personal information collected, data uses, and third-party recipients (16 CFR § 312.4(c)).
- **FR-5.** The system MUST provide parents a dashboard to: (a) review what data has been collected about their child, (b) request deletion of their child's data, and (c) revoke consent at any time (16 CFR § 312.6).
- **FR-6.** Upon parental consent revocation, the system MUST freeze the account within 24 hours and initiate data deletion in accordance with the data-retention policy.
- **FR-7.** The system MUST enforce a data-minimization mode for `coppa_minor` accounts: (a) no behavioral profiling data stored beyond session, (b) no data sent to AI models without explicit per-feature opt-in by the consenting parent, (c) no display advertising or cross-site tracking pixels.
- **FR-8.** The system SHOULD support bulk district consent import (CSV: student_id, parent_email, consent_date, consent_method) for district-wide rollouts.
- **FR-9.** All consent records MUST be immutable once created; amendments create new records referencing the prior record.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — Consent-check middleware MUST add < 2 ms overhead per authenticated request (cached in Redis or in-memory LRU).
- **Security** — Parental consent emails MUST use signed links with 72-hour expiry; link tokens MUST be stored hashed in the database. Consent download archives for minors require step-up re-authentication. See `server/src/http_auth.rs`.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — Full conformance with COPPA 15 U.S.C. §§ 6501–6506 and 16 CFR Part 312: §312.3 (prohibition on collection without consent), §312.4 (direct notice), §312.5 (parental consent methods), §312.6 (rights of parents), §312.7 (confidentiality & security), §312.8 (data retention and deletion), §312.10 (safe harbor programs). NIST 800-53 controls: AC-2 (Account Management), IP-1 (Consent), IP-2 (Individual Access), IP-3 (Information Correction). Student data MUST NOT be transmitted to external AI processors for `coppa_minor` accounts unless the parent has explicitly enabled per-feature AI access.
- **Accessibility** — Consent notice and parent dashboard MUST meet WCAG 2.1 AA (10.7). Plain-language reading level MUST target 6th grade Flesch-Kincaid (see 11.6).
- **Scalability** — Consent-status cache must handle 100k active minor accounts without cache-miss spikes on login.
- **Reliability** — Consent email delivery MUST be retried up to 3 times with exponential backoff via the job queue (17.3).
- **Observability** — Metrics: `coppa_consents_pending_total`, `coppa_consents_approved_total`, `coppa_consents_revoked_total`. Alert if pending queue > 500 for > 24 hours.
- **Maintainability** — COPPA logic in `server/src/services/coppa.rs`; middleware in `server/src/middleware/coppa_gate.rs`.
- **Internationalization** — Consent notices MUST be available in Spanish and English for v1 (see 11.1).
- **Backward compatibility** — Existing adult accounts unaffected; `coppa_minor` defaults to `false` for existing users.

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* a new account is created with DOB indicating age < 13, *when* the registration completes, *then* the account is placed in `pending_consent` status, a consent-notice email is sent to the parent email address, and the student can only view a "waiting for parent permission" screen until consent is granted.
- **AC-2.** *Given* a parent receives the consent email and clicks the signed consent link, *when* the parent reviews and approves, *then* the student account is activated, the consent record is written to `compliance.coppa_consents`, and the parent receives a confirmation email.
- **AC-3.** *Given* a district IT admin uploads a bulk consent CSV with valid records, *when* the import is processed, *then* all matched student accounts are activated and each consent record is tagged with `consent_method = 'school_authorization'`.
- **AC-4.** *Given* a parent revokes consent from the dashboard, *when* revocation is confirmed, *then* the student account is frozen within 24 hours, AI-processing flags are disabled, and a deletion job is queued per the data-retention policy.
- **AC-5.** *Given* a `coppa_minor` account is active with no parent AI opt-in, *when* the student attempts to use any AI-powered feature, *then* the request is blocked with a friendly message and the event is logged without the student's request content.
- **AC-6.** *Given* a compliance export is requested covering 90 days, *when* the export is generated, *then* every consent event (granted, revoked, amended) appears with method, timestamp, and parent email hash.

## 8. Data Model

```sql
-- Migration: 074_coppa.sql

ALTER TABLE user.users
  ADD COLUMN coppa_minor              BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
  ADD COLUMN coppa_consent_status     TEXT NOT NULL DEFAULT 'not_required'
    CHECK (coppa_consent_status IN ('not_required','pending','approved','revoked')),
  ADD COLUMN parent_email             TEXT,
  ADD COLUMN date_of_birth            DATE;

CREATE TABLE IF NOT EXISTS compliance.coppa_consents (
  id                UUID PRIMARY KEY DEFAULT gen_random_uuid(),
  org_id            UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES org.organizations(id),
  student_id        UUID NOT NULL REFERENCES user.users(id),
  parent_email      TEXT NOT NULL,
  consent_method    TEXT NOT NULL CHECK (consent_method IN ('email_signed','school_authorization','upload','direct')),
  consent_token_hash TEXT,          -- hashed signed link token
  consented_at      TIMESTAMPTZ,
  revoked_at        TIMESTAMPTZ,
  prior_record_id   UUID REFERENCES compliance.coppa_consents(id),
  ai_features_enabled BOOLEAN NOT NULL DEFAULT FALSE,
  created_at        TIMESTAMPTZ NOT NULL DEFAULT NOW()
);

CREATE INDEX idx_coppa_consents_student ON compliance.coppa_consents(student_id, revoked_at NULLS FIRST);
```

## 9. API Surface

| Method | Path | Auth Scope | Notes |
|--------|------|-----------|-------|
| `GET` | `/api/v1/compliance/coppa/status` | student (self) | Returns current consent status |
| `POST` | `/api/v1/compliance/coppa/consent-token` | public (signed link) | Parent confirms consent via email link |
| `GET` | `/api/v1/compliance/coppa/parent-dashboard` | parent role | View child data summary |
| `DELETE` | `/api/v1/compliance/coppa/consent/:id` | parent role | Revoke consent |
| `POST` | `/api/v1/compliance/coppa/bulk-import` | org admin | CSV bulk school-authorization import |
| `PATCH` | `/api/v1/compliance/coppa/ai-opt-in` | parent role | Toggle per-feature AI opt-in for minor |

Rate limit: 5 consent-token attempts per 15-minute window per IP.

## 10. UI / UX

- **Registration flow** — age gate: collect DOB; if < 13, redirect to "parent consent required" page; collect parent email.
- **Pending-consent screen** — student-facing holding page with mascot/child-friendly illustration; "Ask your parent to check their email."
- **Parent consent email** — plain-language notice; approve / learn more / decline buttons; links to privacy policy.
- **Parent consent form** — web page with data-collection disclosure table; confirm/deny; accessibility compliant.
- **Parent dashboard** — view collected data categories, toggle AI features, revoke consent button.
- Empty states: "Your child's account is ready" after consent.
- Error states: "This link has expired. Request a new consent email."
- All new pages WCAG 2.1 AA; child-facing UI reading level ≤ 6th grade.
- i18n keys prefixed `coppa.*`; English + Spanish v1.

## 11. AI / ML Considerations

All AI-powered features (adaptive quiz, RAG notebook, AI grading) MUST check `coppa_minor` and `ai_features_enabled` before any prompt construction. The PII redaction proxy (19.11) MUST treat minor accounts as highest sensitivity. No minor's data (name, response text, engagement log) may appear in any AI prompt unless `ai_features_enabled = true` on the active consent record.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| DOB not collected at signup for existing users | H | M | Flag as `not_required` for existing; prompt for DOB on next login for K12 tenants |
| Consent email goes to spam; student stuck | M | H | SPF/DKIM/DMARC setup; resend link in UI; school admin override path |
| District bulk CSV contains errors | M | M | Validate CSV before processing; dry-run mode with error report |
| Parent revocation leaves student mid-assignment | L | M | Grace period: 24h freeze window; notify instructor |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- Feature flag: `coppa_workflow_enabled` — default `false`.
- Phase 1: schema migration; default `not_required` for all existing users.
- Phase 2: backend consent workflow + email templates; enable for K12 pilot tenant.
- Phase 3: student registration DOB gate + parent dashboard.
- Phase 4: AI gate middleware for `coppa_minor` accounts.
- GA criteria: zero consent-pending accounts older than 45 days in pilot cohort.
- Rollback: flag off; schema additive.

## 16. Test Plan

- **Unit** — Token hashing, DOB-to-minor classification, bulk CSV validation.
- **Integration** — Full registration-to-consent flow; consent revocation triggers account freeze within 24h.
- **End-to-end** — Playwright: child registers → holding screen shown → parent clicks email link → account activated.
- **Security** — Consent token replay attack; expired token rejected; OWASP ASVS 4.0 §6 (Stored Cryptography).
- **Accessibility** — axe-core on registration flow and parent dashboard; reading-level check on consent notice text.
- **Performance / load** — 10k concurrent consent-status cache hits; no database call required.
