## 1. Problem Statement

K-12 procurement evaluators ask "Is it on the App Store?" before any other question about mobile strategy. Self-learners discover apps through App Store search. Without an App Store (Apple) and Google Play listing, Lextures cannot be distributed via MDM (Apple School Manager, Google for Education), cannot be found by prospective students browsing education apps, and loses credibility in RFP responses. This feature covers the submission, review, and ongoing maintenance of the iOS App Store and Google Play listings.

## 2. Goals

- Submit the iOS app (7.1) to the Apple App Store in the Education category.
- Submit the Android app (7.1) to the Google Play Store in the Education category.
- Create optimized store listings (screenshots, descriptions, privacy labels, app icons).
- Pass App Store and Google Play review processes, including privacy policy compliance.
- Establish a release pipeline for app updates.

## 3. Non-Goals

- Amazon Appstore or Samsung Galaxy Store listings — deferred.
- App Store Optimization (ASO) marketing campaign — this plan covers technical submission, not growth marketing.
- In-app purchases via App Store / Play Store payment — deferred (payments are via 15.3 Stripe on web).
- Custom white-label app builds per district — deferred (MDM managed config covers branding in V1).

## 4. Personas & User Stories

- **As a district IT administrator**, I want to find Lextures in Apple School Manager so that I can push it to all district iPads and iPhones.
- **As a self-learner browsing the App Store**, I want to find Lextures by searching "adaptive learning" so that I can download it and start learning.
- **As a product manager**, I want an app store listing with accurate screenshots and descriptions so that the download-to-signup conversion rate is maximized.
- **As an engineer**, I want a CI/CD pipeline for app store releases so that app updates ship within 24h of a new build.

## 5. Functional Requirements

- **FR-1.** The system MUST submit an iOS app binary to App Store Connect that passes Apple's review guidelines, including: no use of undocumented APIs, accurate privacy nutrition labels, compliance with App Store Review Guideline 1.0 (privacy) and 5.1 (legal).
- **FR-2.** The system MUST submit an Android APK/AAB to Google Play Console that passes the Play Store review process, including: Data Safety form accuracy, target API level ≥34, and Play policy compliance.
- **FR-3.** The system MUST publish an App Privacy Policy URL (from 20.1) in both store listings.
- **FR-4.** The system MUST provide 5+ App Store screenshots at required resolutions (6.5" iPhone, 12.9" iPad) and 5+ Google Play screenshots (phone, 7" tablet) showing key student flows.
- **FR-5.** The system MUST configure Apple School Manager (Volume Purchase Program) availability so the app can be distributed via MDM.
- **FR-6.** The system SHOULD set up GitHub Actions / Fastlane automated build and submission pipelines for both stores.
- **FR-7.** The system SHOULD configure App Store Connect's TestFlight for internal and external beta testing before each release.
- **FR-8.** The system MAY create a progressive web app store listing via Microsoft Edge Add-ons store as an additional distribution channel.

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

- **Performance** — app binary size must be ≤75 MB on iOS and ≤100 MB on Android to avoid cellular download warnings; optimize with code splitting and asset compression.
- **Security** — production app signed with distribution certificate (iOS) and release keystore (Android); certificates stored in CI secrets (not in repo). No debug builds submitted to stores.
- **Privacy & Compliance** — iOS App Privacy labels must accurately reflect data collected (usage data, user content); Google Play Data Safety form must match. COPPA age gating: app rated 4+ (iOS) and "Everyone" (Android) with no age gate — school-managed distribution assumes parental consent handled by school. FERPA: app privacy policy must document FERPA compliance.
- **Accessibility** — App Store listing screenshots must have alt text (App Store Connect supports this); accessibility statement in app description.
- **Scalability** — N/A for store listing itself.
- **Reliability** — Fastlane pipeline must alert on submission failure; manual review step before auto-submit.
- **Observability** — App Store Connect analytics and Google Play Console analytics tracked; crash rates from store review dashboards monitored.
- **Maintainability** — `Fastfile` and `.github/workflows/app-release.yml` owned by mobile team; yearly certificate renewal calendar reminder.
- **Internationalization** — App store listing localized for at least English and Spanish (largest US K-12 non-English segment).
- **Backward compatibility** — store update submissions must not break users on older app versions during rollout (staged rollout on Play Store).

## 7. Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1.** *Given* the iOS app binary is submitted, *When* Apple completes its review (typically 24–48h), *Then* the app is approved and available in the App Store Education category.
- **AC-2.** *Given* the App Store listing is live, *When* a district IT admin searches "Lextures" in Apple School Manager, *Then* the app appears and can be purchased (free) for deployment.
- **AC-3.** *Given* a Fastlane build pipeline, *When* a `git tag v1.x.x` is pushed, *Then* a release build is automatically submitted to TestFlight (iOS) and Play Store internal testing (Android) within 30 minutes.
- **AC-4.** *Given* the Google Play listing, *When* the Data Safety form is viewed, *Then* it accurately states that educational data is transmitted to Lextures servers only, not sold or used for advertising.
- **AC-5.** *Given* the app is live on the App Store, *When* a user taps "Get", installs, and opens the app, *Then* the login screen appears and the user can sign in with their existing Lextures account.
- **AC-6.** *Given* a staged Play Store rollout at 10%, *When* no crash rate spike is detected after 24h, *Then* rollout can be promoted to 100%.

## 8. Data Model

No server-side data model changes. New CI configuration files:
- `.github/workflows/app-release.yml` — build + submit pipeline.
- `fastlane/Fastfile` — lane definitions for iOS and Android.
- `fastlane/metadata/` — store listing text, screenshots, keywords.

## 9. API Surface

New API endpoints to support store compliance:
- `GET /privacy-policy` — public page (20.1) linked from store listing.
- `GET /app-ads.txt` — if applicable for ASO compliance.

Server: `X-App-Version` header (from 7.1) enables version-specific server behavior for old clients.

## 10. UI / UX

Store listing assets to create:
- App icon: 1024×1024 px (iOS), 512×512 px (Android), no alpha channel, no rounded corners (stores apply their own).
- Screenshots: key flows — login screen, course dashboard, quiz in progress, gradebook, discussion thread.
- Preview video (optional): 15–30 s screencast of a student completing a quiz.
- Short description: "The adaptive LMS for students and educators. Personalized learning, smart quizzes, and instant feedback — anywhere, anytime."
- Long description: feature highlights, org branding mention, support email.
- Keywords (iOS): "LMS, education, adaptive learning, quiz, gradebook, K12, school, learning."

Screenshot production tooling: Fastlane `snapshot` for automated screenshot generation on simulators.

## 12. Integration Points

- 7.1 (Native mobile apps) — app binary being submitted.
- 7.3 (PWA) — PWA distribution as alternative to store (no review needed for web install).
- 20.1 (Privacy policy) — URL linked in store listings.
- GitHub Actions + Fastlane — CI/CD pipeline.
- App Store Connect API + Google Play Developer API — automated submission.

## 13. Dependencies & Sequencing

- Must ship after: 7.1 (Native apps must exist before submission), 20.1 (Privacy policy must be live before Apple review).
- Must ship before: district MDM deployment; self-learner growth campaigns.
- Shared infra needed: Apple Developer Program membership ($99/year); Google Play Developer account ($25 one-time); code-signing certificates in CI secrets.

## 14. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Apple App Store review rejection | M | H | Pre-review against Apple guidelines; respond to rejections within 24h; appeal if needed |
| Distribution certificate expiry causes app build failure | M | M | Set calendar reminder 60 days before expiry; automate with Fastlane Match |
| App Privacy label inaccuracy triggers Google enforcement | L | H | Legal/compliance review of Data Safety form before submission |
| Binary size exceeds cellular threshold | M | M | Tree-shake unused dependencies; lazy-load large modules |

## 15. Rollout Plan

- No feature flag — app store release is the rollout.
- Sequence: prepare store assets → TestFlight beta → App Store submission → Apple review → launch day → Google Play staged rollout (10% → 50% → 100%).
- Dogfood: TestFlight invite to team + 5 pilot school students.
- GA criteria: both stores live; no crashes in 48h of staged Android rollout; ASM availability confirmed.
- Rollback: pause rollout on Play Store; expedited review on App Store (rare); hotfix release.

## 16. Test Plan

- **Unit** — Fastlane pipeline dry-run (build but don't submit).
- **Integration** — TestFlight distribution verified with 10 test users.
- **End-to-end** — full student journey on TestFlight build (login → quiz → grade).
- **Security** — confirm no debug symbols in release build; certificate validity check.
- **Accessibility** — App Store screenshot alt text present.
- **Performance / load** — binary size check in CI (fail if >75 MB iOS / >100 MB Android).
- **Manual exploratory** — install from TestFlight on multiple device models; verify login and core flows.
